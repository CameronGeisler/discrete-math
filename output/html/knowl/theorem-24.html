Theorem 6.2.2. Sum of the first n squares of natural numbers.

The sum of the first \(n\) squares of natural numbers is given by,

\begin{equation*} \frac{n(n + 1)(2n + 1)}{6} \end{equation*}

In other words,

\begin{equation*} 1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 + \cdots + (n - 1)^2 + n^2 = \sum_{i=1}^n ^2 = \frac{n(n + 1)(2n + 1)}{6} \end{equation*}
