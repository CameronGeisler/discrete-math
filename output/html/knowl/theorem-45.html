Theorem 7.4.1. De Morgan's laws.

Let \(A, B \) be sets. Then,

\begin{equation*} \overline{A \cap B} = \overline{A} \cup \overline{B} \qquad \text{and} \qquad \overline{A \cup B} = \overline{A} \cap \overline{B} \end{equation*}

In other words,

  • \(x \notin A \cup B\) if and only if \(x \notin A\) and \(x \notin B\text{.}\)

  • \(x \notin A \cap B\) if and only if \(x \notin A\) or \(x \notin B\)
